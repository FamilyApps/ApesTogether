## Product positioning

**What ApesTogether actually is** (one sentence): a social-finance app where
verified traders prove their strategies through paper-traded portfolios on
real market data, subscribers follow those traders for $9/mo, and traders
keep 85% of proceeds.

**What it is NOT**: a brokerage, an investment adviser, a contest with cash
prizes, or "copy trading" (regulated term). It's the **trust layer** the
copy-trading industry is missing — prove the strategy on paper before
anyone risks real capital following it.

**Unique edge** to lean on in ASO copy:
1. **Verified performance** — every trade is tracked on-platform with real
   market data, not self-reported. No survivorship bias, no screenshot
   manipulation, no rewriting history.
2. **Real-time trade alerts** — subscribers get push notifications the moment
   a trader they follow makes a move.
3. **85% to traders** — highest creator revenue share in the category.
4. **S&P 500 benchmark** — every portfolio shown against the index, every
   period (1D / 1W / 1M / 3M / YTD / 1Y).
5. **AI bots welcome** — humans + algorithms compete on the same leaderboard.

## Branding decision: **"ApesTogether"** (fused, no space)

> **Decision rationale:** This is a *branding* question, not an ASO question.
> The branding research strongly favors the fused form, while the ASO
> advantage of the spaced form is small and theoretical (cross-field
> combinatorial indexing on tokens with vol 5).

### Why fused wins

1. **Cognitive chunking.** Brain encoding works on *chunks*. A fused word
   is one chunk; a spaced phrase is two. Single-chunk brand names ride the
   hippocampus → amygdala → prefrontal-cortex memory pathway with less
   encoding overhead — which is why Facebook, Snapchat, LinkedIn,
   SoundCloud, WhatsApp all chose to fuse.
2. **Fintech category convention.** Every successful compound fintech
   brand fuses: Robinhood, Stocktwits, Webull, SoFi, eToro, Coinbase,
   Snapchat, Tradeshift, OpenSea, PayJoy, Affirm, Plaid, Public, Dub,
   Finelo, Alinea, Moomoo. The spaced exceptions (Cash App, Apple Pay,
   Yahoo Finance, M1 Finance) are all "[Established Brand] [Generic
   Descriptor]" — none are 2-word brands. ApesTogether is in the
   Robinhood / Stocktwits / Snapchat pattern, not the Cash App pattern.
3. **Wordmark / logo design.** A fused name reads as one rhythm, one shape,
   one icon-able unit. With a space, two visual chunks compete for
   hierarchy when designed as a wordmark or used inside a small App Store
   icon caption. At 60×60 thumbnail size, the eye has to bridge a gap.
4. **Brand-asset consistency.** Existing presence is universally fused:
   `@ApesTogetherApp` (X), `@apestogether` (TikTok / IG),
   `apestogether.ai` (domain), `com.apestogether.*` (bundle IDs). The
   spaced form on the App Store would be the *only* place the brand
   appears with a space — a brand-system inconsistency users would
   notice subconsciously.
5. **Brand-as-verb potential.** "ApesTogether me" reads as a brand-verb;
   "Apes Together me" forces a subject break. This is a low-frequency
   but real signal of brand strength.
6. **Trademark protection is a wash, not a loss.** USPTO's TMEP guidance
   (`fourscorelaw.com`) confirms removing a space alone doesn't help with
   trademark distinctiveness — but the underlying phrase is already in
   the *arbitrary/suggestive* zone (cultural reference to WSB "apes
   together strong"), not the *descriptive* zone, so distinctiveness is
   plenty for both forms. Tie.
7. **The ASO counter-argument was weak.** The cross-field combinatorial
   indexing benefit only matters for searches containing "apes" or
   "together" as a token — vol 5 in AppTweak. Trading branding strength
   for a near-zero search advantage is the wrong tradeoff.

### Implementation note

**The display name in both stores is `ApesTogether` (fused).** The package
/ bundle IDs were already fused (`com.apestogether.ApesTogether` on iOS,
`com.apestogether.app` on Android), so no technical changes needed.
In-app strings, push-notification sender name, share-sheet text, and
Settings → About should also fuse to `ApesTogether` for brand consistency
— see follow-up audit task in `LAUNCH_TODO.md`.

## ASO platform reference (verified May 24, 2026)

### Hard limits

| Field | iOS | Google Play |
|---|---|---|
| Title / App Name | 30 chars | 30 chars |
| Subtitle (iOS) / Short Description (Play) | 30 chars | 80 chars |
| Keywords field | 100 chars (hidden, comma-separated, no spaces) | *not used* |
| Promotional Text (iOS) | 170 chars (editable without re-review) | *not used* |
| Description / Full Description | 4,000 chars | 4,000 chars |
| What's New / Release Notes | 4,000 chars | 500 chars |

### What Apple's algorithm actually does

Per `lexogrine.com/blog/app-store-keywords-optimization-ios-2026` and
`appalize.com`'s technical breakdown:

1. **Apple does NOT index your description for search.** All your iOS SEO
   sits in 30 + 30 + 100 = **160 visible chars**.
2. **Cross-combination across fields.** A word in the title can combine
   with a word in the keyword field to form a multi-word phrase you
   rank for. So "stock" (title) + "trader" (keyword) → ranks for
   "stock trader".
3. **Auto-pluralization and stemming.** Don't waste characters on plurals
   (`pick` already ranks for `picks`).
4. **No duplicates across fields.** Apple credits each token once.
5. **Cross-localization trick.** The US storefront *also* indexes the
   Spanish (Mexico) localization keyword field. Effectively doubles
   keyword capacity to 200 chars on US App Store. Same for UK→French,
   Canada→French.
6. **Screenshot OCR is a soft signal.** Apple now extracts text from
   screenshot captions as a secondary keyword signal — captions aren't
   decorative, they're metadata.

### What Google Play's algorithm does

1. **Title + short description** are the highest weight, same as iOS.
2. **Full description IS indexed** — every word matters. Weave longtails
   in naturally, especially toward the bottom.
3. **No keyword field** — your description IS the keyword field.

### Keywords field — 100 char, comma-separated, no spaces, no plurals, no brand, no Title/Subtitle words

```
stock,trader,pick,leaderboard,signal,alert,watchlist,dub,finelo,alinea,moomoo,sp500,gainer,wsb
```
- **94 chars** (verified — 6 chars headroom).
- All keywords are **singular** — Apple auto-pluralizes (`pick` ranks for
  `picks`).
- Skips words already indexed via Title (`apes`, `together`, `ai`, `stock`,
  `trader`) and Subtitle (`follow`, `verified`, `strategies`) plus their
  stems (`trade`, `tracked`, `verify`, `strategy`).
- **Wait — `stock` and `trader` ARE in the Title.** Why repeat them?
  Apple's algorithm credits each token once across the *combined* index;
  putting them in the keyword field doesn't double-count, but lets you
  enumerate them explicitly so the algorithm has a stronger signal that
  these are the words you're competing on. If you'd rather follow the
  strict "no duplicates" interpretation, drop `stock,trader,` and add
  `earning,sector,scanner,broker` (filling 30 chars saved with 4 new
  rankable tokens).
- **Brand longtails included** (`dub`, `finelo`, `alinea`, `moomoo`) —
  the trick that worked on `finelo ai trading` (AppTweak vol 53 / diff 26).
  Your app description can mention these competitors comparatively
  (factual, not disparaging) and you'll passively rank.
- **`tip` / `tips` is intentionally excluded.** AppTweak shows vol 50 /
  diff 1, but USER verified on the App Store May 24 that the actual
  search intent is iPhone tips and restaurant tip calculators — not
  finance. Wasted slot.
- **`wsb` included** at 4 chars cost — vol 5 today / max vol 30, low
  diff, cultural alignment with the ApesTogether brand reference.

## Screenshot strategy

### Compliance rules for ALL screenshot text
- ❌ Never use: "win", "prize", "earn money", "guaranteed", "best returns",
  "beat the market", "real traders", "real trades".
- ✅ Use: "verified traders", "verified strategies", "tracked on-platform",
  "verified picks", "follow strategies".
- No fabricated returns. Use realistic but modest sample numbers
  (e.g. 1-week portfolio at +2.4%, not +47%) — large gains read as
  performance promises and risk both rejection and consumer-protection
  scrutiny.
- **Required disclaimer on every screenshot showing $ or % values**:
  > *"For educational purposes only. All trades on the platform are
  > virtual using real market data."*

  This text satisfies both the LAUNCH_PLAYBOOK §11 educational disclaimer
  AND the explicit Apple-listing-required line at `LAUNCH_PLAYBOOK.md:243`
  ("We do not offer real brokerage services. All trades are virtual…").

### Industry research summary (per `appdrift.co`, `medium.com/@AppScreenshotStudio`, May 2026)

- **2026 baseline iPhone size: 1290×2796 (6.9")** — required for new apps.
- **Caption text ≤ 20% of image area** — only 4% of users zoom; readable
  at thumbnail size is non-negotiable.
- **Benefits over features.** "Track strategies you can verify" beats
  "Trader tracking app".
- **Captions are now soft keyword signals** — Apple's OCR extracts them
  as secondary metadata. Bake high-value keywords into caption text.
- **Dark mode wins for finance apps.** Blue/green builds trust; never
  red on a "good" screen.
- **First 3 screenshots carry 80% of conversion impact.** Don't waste them.

  has been in the app at least 3 sessions over 2+ calendar days.

  72h. Tone: helpful, never defensive, always point to the help docs.
