The core difference: who pulls the trigger?

All three apps answer the same question — "what are better investors than me doing right now?" — but they differ on what happens next.

Dub and eToro's CopyTrader execute for you. You allocate money to a portfolio or investor, and the platform mirrors their moves automatically inside an account you hold with them. It's convenient, but you give up trade-by-trade control and you must move money onto their platform.

ApesTogether alerts you and stops there. When a trader you subscribe to buys or sells, you get a real-time push notification with the ticker, action, and position size. You then decide — in your own brokerage account, with your own money — whether to follow, size differently, or skip it. Nothing executes automatically, and ApesTogether never holds your funds.

ApesTogether

ApesTogether is a leaderboard of traders whose performance is built exclusively from timestamped trades logged in the app — there's no way to import a track record or claim past wins, which means every percentage you see was earned in the open. AI-managed portfolios trade on the same leaderboard as humans, so the standings double as a running experiment: can retail traders actually beat the machines?

  • Free to browse the leaderboard and follow the AI-vs-human standings
  • $9/month to subscribe to an individual trader's real-time trade alerts
  • Any user can monetize their own track record — traders keep 85% of net subscription proceeds
  • Not a broker: no funds held, no trades executed, no financial advice
Best for: investors who want ideas and transparency but insist on keeping their own brokerage and making the final call — and traders who want to get paid for a verified track record.

Dub

Dub is a copy-trading brokerage: you browse portfolios (including ones tracking well-known investors and institutional filings), allocate money, and Dub's brokerage executes the copies automatically. It's the most hands-off option of the three.

  • Fully automatic portfolio copying inside a Dub brokerage account
  • Registered broker-dealer; your money is on their platform
  • Subscription pricing plus fees on certain portfolios
Best for: hands-off investors who are comfortable funding a dedicated account and want copying handled entirely for them.

eToro

eToro is a full social brokerage — one of the oldest names in copy trading globally. In the U.S. you can trade stocks, ETFs, and crypto, follow other investors' feeds, and use CopyTrader to automatically mirror a popular investor with an allocation of your choosing.

  • Self-directed trading and automatic copying in one platform
  • Large international community and long-running Popular Investor program
  • U.S. product has a narrower asset lineup than the international version
Best for: investors who want a single all-in-one brokerage with a social layer, and don't mind that copying happens on eToro's platform rather than their existing account.

Which one should you pick?

It comes down to control and where your money lives. If you want zero effort and don't mind moving funds, Dub is the most automated. If you want a full brokerage with social features, eToro is the most complete. If you want to keep your existing brokerage, see exactly what verified traders (and AI portfolios) are doing in real time, and stay the final decision-maker on every trade — that's ApesTogether. And if you're a trader with a track record worth following, ApesTogether is the only one of the three built to pay you for it from day one.

FAQ

Common questions

Is ApesTogether a copy-trading app like Dub or eToro?
+

Not exactly. Dub and eToro's CopyTrader execute trades for you automatically inside accounts you hold with them. ApesTogether sends you real-time notifications when a verified trader makes a move — you then decide whether to act in your own brokerage account. Nothing is executed automatically and ApesTogether never holds your money.

Does ApesTogether hold my money or execute trades?
+

No. ApesTogether is not a broker-dealer or investment advisor. It provides information — verified trade activity and real-time alerts. All actual trading happens in your own brokerage account, under your control.

How are track records verified on ApesTogether?
+

Performance is computed only from timestamped trades logged in the app from the moment a trader joins. There is no way to import a history or self-report past results, so every track record on the leaderboard was built in the open.

What does ApesTogether cost compared to Dub and eToro?
+

Browsing the ApesTogether leaderboard is free; subscribing to an individual trader's real-time alerts costs $9/month. Dub charges a platform subscription plus fees on some portfolios, and eToro is fee-free to copy but applies spreads and minimum copy amounts. Always check each provider's current pricing.

Can I earn money as a trader on these platforms?
+

On ApesTogether, any user can monetize their verified track record and keeps 85% of net subscription proceeds. Dub and eToro both have creator programs, but they are limited to selected or top-tier investors. ApesTogether is the only one of the three where monetization is open to everyone from day one.

What is the AI-vs-human leaderboard on ApesTogether?
+

AI-managed portfolios trade on the same leaderboard as human traders under identical verification rules. The standings show, in real time, whether human traders are beating the AI portfolios — a running experiment you can watch (or join) for free.
